# 22.6 Heroin (Afghan Drug Crisis)

## Model Metadata

- **Simulation Period**: Student-defined
- **Time Step**: Student-defined
- **Integration Method**: Euler

## Description
Model of the Afghan heroin/opium crisis and European drug market. Explores opium licensing for medical purposes as alternative to eradication-only counter-narcotics strategy.

## Context
- European heroin price: €10–50/gram (2007)
- Afghanistan: ~90% of world opium production
- 13 million Afghans in drug trade, 2.3 million in cultivation
- Opium licensing proposed by European Parliament (2006)

## Key Model Components
- Afghan opium production (poppy cultivation dynamics)
- Drug trafficking chain (production → processing → distribution)
- European heroin market (supply, demand, price)
- Alternative livelihood programs
- Eradication vs licensing policy options
- HIV/AIDS epidemic connection
- Diamorphine (medical heroin) shortage in Europe

## Conceptual Stocks
- poppy_cultivation_area

## Policy Scenarios to Test
1. Eradication-only strategy
2. Opium licensing for medical morphine production
3. Combined approach
4. Alternative livelihood programs
5. Demand reduction in Europe

## Sensitivity Analysis
- Vary eradication effectiveness
- Vary licensing program scope
- Vary European demand elasticity
- Test impact on Afghan farmer decision-making
